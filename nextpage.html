Morocco at a Crossroads of Worlds

Located in North Africa, Morocco borders both the Atlantic Ocean and the Mediterranean Sea. It connects Africa to Europe, and the Arab world to Amazigh heritage.

Mountains, deserts, and coastlines shaped its history — and its dynasties.

History and Geography

1. The Phoenicians & Romans

Before the Islamic era, the Phoenicians established coastal trading posts like Lixus. Later, the Romans occupied northern Morocco, leaving behind the ruins of Volubilis.

2. The Idrisid Dynasty (788-974)

Founded by Idris I, this was the first major Islamic dynasty in Morocco. They founded Fes, the nation's spiritual heart.

3. The Almoravid Dynasty (1040–1147)

A Saharan Amazigh empire that founded Marrakech and united Morocco with Al-Andalus.

4. The Almohad Dynasty (1121–1269)

Reformers who built the Hassan Tower and the Giralda.

5. The Marinid Dynasty (1244–1465)

Patrons of art and education who built the famous Madrasas of Fes.

6. The Saadi Dynasty (1549–1659)

Defeated the Portuguese and built the El Badi Palace.

7. The Alaouite Dynasty (1666–Present)

The current reigning family who unified modern Morocco.
