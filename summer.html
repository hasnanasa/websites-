Summer in Morocco

Sun, Surf, and Sapphire Seas

Inspiring Coastal Gems

Al Hoceima

The "Mediterranean Pearl." Famous for Quemado Beach and turquoise waters nestled between the Rif Mountains.

Taghazout

The surf capital. A bohemian fishing village where the Atlantic breeze keeps the summer heat perfectly cool.

Dakhla Lagoon

Where the Sahara meets the sea. A world-class spot for kitesurfing and eating fresh oysters under the desert sun.

The Summer Spirit

While the interior cities like Marrakech bask in intense gold heat, the coast becomes a playground. Summer is the season of Grilled Sardines at the port, Fresh Mint Tea on the beach, and the late-night Corniche walks. It is the time for festivals like the *Gnaoua World Music Festival* in Essaouira, where the music flows as freely as the ocean waves.

Al Hoceima (Mediterranean)

Summer Detail: Crystal clear turquoise bays. Perfect for cliff diving and boat trips to the rocky "Bokkoya" coastline.

Essaouira (The Windy City)

Summer Detail: A sanctuary from the heat. Famous for the Gnaoua music festival and cool Atlantic breezes.

Dakhla Lagoon

Summer Detail: Where the Sahara meets the Sea. A world-class destination for kitesurfers and nature lovers.

Asilah (The Arts Town)

Summer Detail: Famous for its annual mural painting festival. A peaceful, white-washed town with golden beaches.
