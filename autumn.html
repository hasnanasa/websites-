Autumn in Morocco

The Season of the Golden Sun

Inspiring Autumn Destinations

Erfoud & The Tafilalet

The Harvest: October is the time for the "Date Festival." Millions of palms offer their sweet golden fruit.

The Colors: The walnut and cherry trees turn bright yellow and orange against the red soil of the mountains.

Taliouine

Saffron Season: In late October, the purple saffron flowers bloom. It is the most expensive spice harvest in the world.

The Autumn Vibe

As the "Chergui" (hot wind) stops, a peaceful calm falls over Morocco. Autumn is the season of **Pomegranates** and **Quinces**. It is the best time for Sahara trekking because the sand is cool and the light is soft gold—perfect for photography. People return to the Hammams to prepare for the coming winter, and the smell of roasting chickpeas and nuts fills the street corners.

Taliouine (Saffron Hills)

Autumn Detail: The harvest of the world’s most expensive spice. Experience the fields turning purple at dawn for just two weeks in October.

Erfoud (The Oasis)

Autumn Detail: The Date Festival. A celebration of the "Mejhoul" date, where the oases turn deep gold under the desert sun.

Imilchil (High Atlas)

Autumn Detail: The legendary Marriage Festival. A time of Berber poetry, tribal music, and ancient traditions in the cool mountain air.

The Walnut Valleys

Autumn Detail: Watch the mountain forests change from green to fiery orange and yellow as the walnut and apple harvests begin.
