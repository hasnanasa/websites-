Winter in Morocco

Snowy Peaks & Warm Tea

Inspiring Winter Towns

Ifrane

The "Little Switzerland" of Morocco. Famous for its red-roofed houses covered in thick white snow.

Oukaïmeden

The highest ski resort in Africa. Ski down the slopes with a view of the Marrakech plains far below.

The Sahara

Surprisingly, winter is the best time for the desert. Warm days and freezing, star-filled nights.

The Warmth of Winter

While the mountains are white, the Moroccan heart stays warm. Winter is the season of Bissara (fava bean soup) and Harira. It is the time for "Almond Blossom Festivals" in the south (Tafraoute) and gathering around the Majmar (charcoal heater) with a glass of hot mint tea.

Ifrane & Azrou

Winter Trait: Alpine architecture and the famous Stone Lion. Visit the Cèdre Gouraud to see macaques in the snow.

Oukaïmeden Resort

Winter Trait: High-altitude skiing in the High Atlas. A unique mix of Berber culture and winter sports.

Tafraoute Valleys

Winter Trait: The Almond Blossom miracle. Witness the pink petals blooming against the red granite rocks in February.

The Sahara Dunes

Winter Trait: Intense blue skies by day and campfire stories under the brightest stars in the world by night.
